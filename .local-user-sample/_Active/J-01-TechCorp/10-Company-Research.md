## Why This Role Exists (Working Hypotheses)

### Hypothesis 1: Platform Consolidation Need
**Signal:** Job description emphasizes "platform" across governance + eval + deployment. But TechCorp's product was historically point-solution focused (strong on eval, weaker on governance).

**Interpretation:** They're trying to build a "full stack" platform so customers don't stitch together 5 tools. Previous PM moved on; they need someone to think systematically about it.

**How to validate:** In interview, ask "What does 'platform' mean to you? Is it about breadth (doing more) or depth (doing the same things better)?"

**Signal:** Job description mentions "regulated industries" and "compliance-heavy customers." That wasn't emphasized in older job postings (I pulled a 2025 version from Wayback Machine).

**Interpretation:** Fortune 500 customers are now asking not just "does your model work?" but "can we audit it? Can we show compliance?" This is a business pressure, not a technology pressure.

**How to validate:** Ask "What's changed in your customer base in the last 12 months? What's the #1 objection you're hearing from procurement?"

### Hypothesis 3: Go-to-Market Weakness
**Signal:** JD emphasizes "go-to-market strategy," which isn't always core for IC principals. Suggests their sales process is struggling. Maybe customers don't understand what to buy, or sales can't close deals without PM involvement.

**Interpretation:** This PM will spend 50% on product strategy, 50% on sales enablement and customer advisory. Not pure product strategy.

**How to validate:** Ask "Walk me through your last closed deal over $1M. Was the PM involved? Where did the process get stuck?"

## Interview Preparation Angles

**Area 1: Depth on governance vs eval**
- They'll likely ask you to articulate the difference
- Be ready with real customer examples (even hypothetical ones)
- Prep: How have you seen governance requirements evolve? What's non-negotiable vs nice-to-have?

**Area 2: Platform thinking at scale**
- They'll probe: Can you think in systems? Or are you a feature person?
- Prep: Come with 1 example of platform strategy you've executed or would execute
- Prep: Understand the difference between "tools used together" and "integrated platform"

**Area 3: Enterprise selling**
- Smaller team than FAANG. PM is involved in sales cycles.
- Prep: Story about how you've worked with sales to close a deal or unblock procurement
- Prep: Comfort with 1-2 hour customer calls, translating technical capability to business value
